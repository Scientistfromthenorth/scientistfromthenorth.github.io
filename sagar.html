07 / About

The Person

About
Me

I grew up believing that mathematics and painting were the same pursuit — both attempts to describe something true about the world in a language the world didn't invent for itself.

I spent my twenties trying to choose between a laboratory and a studio. Eventually I stopped choosing. Today my research group studies how biological networks organise themselves, and my evenings are spent rendering those same networks in oil paint and verse. The two practices have more in common than people expect — both require patience with uncertainty, both are improved by revision, and neither ever feels finished.

I live in Melbourne with my partner and a poorly-behaved cat named Euler. I am interested in collaborations that challenge disciplinary boundaries and in conversations with anyone who has looked at a dataset and seen something beautiful.

Values
◈

Rigour

Beautiful ideas that cannot survive scrutiny are only decorative. I hold my own work to the same standard I apply to others'.

◇

Curiosity

The most interesting questions are always one field to the left of wherever I am standing. I try to stay uncomfortable.

◉

Openness

Science belongs to everyone. Every project I lead produces open code, open data, and accessible writing alongside the technical paper.

✦

Craft

Whether the medium is mathematics, paint, or language — the quality of attention you bring to the work is visible in the result.
